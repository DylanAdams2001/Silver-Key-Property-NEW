Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

H.O.M.E. stands for Hands-Off ownership, Ongoing government-funded income, Managed entirely on your behalf by a registered housing association, and an Enduring 20-25 year lease. It's the model behind every property we bring to investors: you own the asset, the income is structured and government-backed, and someone else runs it day to day. The blueprint walks through exactly how each part works.
Self-contained residential properties adapted for adults who need a level of care or supervision (most commonly people with learning disabilities, autism, mental health conditions, or physical disabilities), let to a registered care or housing provider rather than directly to individual tenants.
The rent is government-funded, paid primarily through Housing Benefit or Universal Credit housing costs to the registered provider, who in turn pays the lease rent to the property owner. The blueprint sets out how this differs from standard buy-to-let income.
These include provider covenant strength, changes to welfare policy or local authority commissioning, property-specific voids between leases, and the general illiquidity of direct property investment. Capital is at risk and returns are not guaranteed. The blueprint covers this in full.
